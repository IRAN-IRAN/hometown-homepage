Visit Isfahan

Isfahan, often referred to as "Half of the World," Isfahan offers visitors a journey through history, art, and culture.

Tourist Attractions of Isfahan

Experiencing beautiful moments by visiting Naqsh-e Jahan Square

A UNESCO World Heritage Site,Constructed between 1598 and 1629,immersing yourself in its rich history, walking through the majestic Naqsh-e Jahan Square.The square is surrounded by structures of different purposes and architecture.

Monar Jonban (Shaking Minarets) is a unique historical site

famous for its two minarets that can be shaken. Built during the Ilkhanid era in the 14th century.when one minaret is shaken, the other one vibrates in sync.The phenomenon has made Monar Jonban a fascinating example of ancient Persian engineering and architecture.

Handicraft exhibitions

Isfahan is renowned for its exquisite handicrafts, which reflect the city’s rich artistic heritage.Khatamkari (Marquetry) – An intricate art form that involves inlaying thin pieces of wood, bone, and metal to create stunning geometric patterns on items.Miniature Paintings – Detailed and colorful small-scale paintings that often depict scenes from Persian history, literature, and mythology.
